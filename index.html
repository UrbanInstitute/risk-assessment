The First Step Act’s Risk Assessment Tool

Who is eligible for early release from federal prison?

Updated MONTH DAY, 2021

The First Step Act offers people behind bars the opportunity to earn credits toward early release from federal prison. To help determine who is eligible, the US Department of Justice created PATTERN, a risk assessment tool that predicts the likelihood that a currently incarcerated person might reoffend. This interactive version of PATTERN shows how each risk factor raises or lowers a person’s risk score and can estimate whether they qualify for early release.

Update: In January 2020, the Federal Bureau of Prisons updated PATTERN, based on public feedback, to add new risk factors and remove others, change how those factors are weighted, and revise the scoring. We’ve updated this tool accordingly.

How to use this tool:

  • Risk factors are divided into two categories: static factors that don’t change over time and dynamic factors that can change.
  • Because the risk factors for reoffending can differ between men and women, scoring varies slightly by gender. Start by selecting a gender. Then select answers for each risk factor.
  • PATTERN calculates two separate scores: a person’s risk of reoffending in general and their risk of reoffending with an act of violence. Each answer below shows the point values for both methods. These scores determine whether someone is in a minimum-, low-, medium-, or high-risk category for general and violent reoffending. Whichever risk category is highest—general or violent—is the one that counts toward eligibility.

About

The Federal Bureau of Prisons (BOP) uses the Prisoner Assessment Tool Targeting Estimated Risk and Needs (PATTERN) to help determine who is eligible for early release. For those not disqualified based on their offenses, eligibility for early release is a function of risk level. Only people who score low or minimum risk are eligible for early transfer from prison, either into home confinement, a halfway house, or community supervision. During the COVID-19 pandemic, BOP has been using the PATTERN score as one factor in determining who is granted early release to home confinement.

Because the risk factors for reoffending can differ by gender, men and women are scored slightly differently. Also, PATTERN assesses two types of risk scores: one for general recidivism and one for violent recidivism. The table below shows how the general and violent risk scores correspond with the general and violent risk categories for men and women. The higher of the two risk categories is used to determine eligibility for early release.

General Violent
Risk category Men Women Men Women
Minimum ≤10 ≤5 ≤6 ≤2
Low 11-30 6-31 7-24 3-19
Medium 31-43 32-49 25-30 20-25
High ≥44 ≥50 ≥31 ≥26

See the DOJ reports from July 2010, January 2020, and June 2020 for more information on scoring and how the tools were developed.

The First Step Act also requires that a person must earn enough time credits to at least equal his or her remaining sentence to be eligible for early release. For release to home confinement or a halfway house, a person must maintain a low or minimum risk score for at least two assessment cycles. Prison wardens can approve early release for anyone, regardless of risk level. BOP must conduct periodic reassessments and include dynamic factors, such as the number of programs completed, so that people can move from higher to lower levels of risk.
